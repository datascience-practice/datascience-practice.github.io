Is it Fair?

Published: Fri 10 April 2015
By Sergey Vlasov

In Intro to Data Science.

Basic conditions

  • Probability of picking a fair coin: p(f) = 0.9

  • Probability of picking Heads given a loaded coin: p(h|l) = 0.9

  • Probability of picking a loaded coin: p(l) = 0.1

  • Probability of picking Heads given a fair coin: p(h|f) = 0.5

Given outcome(sequence): 4H0T

  • seq = 4H0T = HHHH

Fair coin case:

  • probability of the outcome, given a fair coin: p(seq|f) = p(h|f) 4 = 0.54
  • combined probability of picking a fair coin AND getting the sequence: p(seq, f) = p(f) * p(seq|f)

Loaded coin case:

  • probability of the outcome, given a loaded coin: p(seq|l) = p(h|l) 4 = 0.54
  • combined probability of picking a fair coin AND getting the sequence: p(seq, f) = p(f) * p(seq|f)
